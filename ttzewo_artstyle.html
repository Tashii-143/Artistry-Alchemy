Skills to practise to achieve a Ttzewo Artstyle

What is a Ttzewo Artstyle?

TTZEWO style is cute and simplified, with big heads, soft bodies, and expressive faces. Colors are gentle, and the focus is on making characters feel charming and fun. Perfect for beginners who want to create adorable, stylized art.

Core Skills to Practice (Most Important)

Simple Anatomy

Heads are usually big

Bodies are short and soft

Arms and legs are simple shapes

Practice tip:

Draw characters using circles and rectangles before adding details.

Clean Line Art

Lines are smooth

Not too many sketchy lines

Line thickness is usually even

Practise tip:

Do slow lines instead of fast scribbles. Practice drawing circles and curves.

Cute Facial Features

Large eyes

Small nose

Simple mouth (often a line or tiny shape)

Practise tip

Draw 10 different eyes on one page using the same face shape.

Soft Expressions

Happy, shy, calm, or playful

No extreme realism

Practise tip:

Practice changing only the eyebrows and mouth to show emotion.

Supporting Skills (Helpful but Not First)

Soft Coloring

Not too much contrast

Pastel or gentle colors

Practise tip:

Use 2–3 colors per character plus lighter shades.

Simple Shading

Minimal shadows

Shading is soft and smooth

Practise tip:

Add shading to only one side of the face or hair.

Stylized Hair

Hair is drawn in big clumps.

Not individual strands

Practise tips:

Draw hair using 3–5 big shapes, then add a few lines.

What NOT to Focus on Yet

Realistic muscles

Complex backgrounds

Heavy textures

Super detailed clothing

Remember:Keeping things simple is key to this style.

Easy Daily Practice Routine (15–20 Minutes)

5 min – draw heads and faces

5 min – practice eyes

5 min – draw simple bodies

5 min – color one sketch

Remember:Short practice is better than long, tiring sessions.
