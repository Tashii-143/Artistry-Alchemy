Skills to practise to achieve a Jelly Artstyle

What is a Jelly Artstyle

Jelly art looks soft, squishy, and shiny—like your characters are made of candy or gel. Shapes are round, lines are smooth, and colors are bright or pastel. It’s great for creating cute, bouncy, and colorful art.

Core Skills to Practice (Most Important)

Round Shapes

Circles and ovals everywhere

Very few sharp corners

Practise tip:

Fill a page with circles and blobs before drawing characters.

Smooth Line Art

Clean, flowing lines

No sharp angles

Practise tip:

Draw slowly and confidently. Redraw lines instead of sketching over them.

Simple Forms

Bodies are simplified

Details are minimal

Practise tip:

Draw characters using 3–5 big shapes only.

Soft Highlights (Shiny Look)

White or light highlights

Makes the art look “jelly-like”

Practise tip:

Add a small white shine on cheeks, hair, or objects.

Supporting Skills (Helpful but Not Required First)

Bright, Soft Colors

Pastel or candy colors

Colors are not too dark

Practice tip:

Pick one main color and two lighter shades.

Gentle Shading

Smooth shadows

No hard lines between colors

Practise tip:

Shade only one side and blend lightly.

Cute Expressions

Simple eyes

Small mouth

Friendly emotions

Practice tip:

Practice faces with dots for eyes and a tiny mouth.

Common Beginner Mistakes

Too many sharp edges

Too much detail

Dark, heavy colors

Over-shading

Remember: Simple = better for Jelly style.

Easy Daily Practice (15 Minutes)

5 mins - jelly blobs and shapes

5 mins - faces with shiny cheeks

5 mins - color one simple drawing
