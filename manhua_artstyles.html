Skills to practise to achieve a Manhua Artstyle

What is a Manhua Artstyle?

Manhua is the Chinese comic style. It’s elegant and flowy, with soft colors, graceful poses, and smooth line work. Perfect for fantasy or historical stories with a calm and beautiful look.

Core Skills to Practice (Most Important)

Elegant Facial Proportions

Slim faces

Smaller eyes than Manga

Gentle nose and lips

Practise tip:

Draw the head slightly taller than wide and place eyes lower on the face.

Clean, Thin Line Art

Lines are light and smooth

Very few messy sketch lines

Practise tip:

Draw slowly and confidently. Erase extra lines instead of drawing over them.

Graceful Anatomy

Slim bodies

Long arms and legs

Soft, natural poses

Practise tip:

Use simple stick figures first, then add shapes.

Flowing Hair & Clothing

Hair moves smoothly

Clothes look light and flowing

Practise tip:

Draw hair and clothes in large shapes first, then add details.

Supporting Skills (Learn After Core)

Light, gentle colors

Not too bright or dark

Practise tip:

Use pastel or muted colors.

Light Shading

Minimal shadows

Smooth blends

Practise tip:

Shade lightly under hair, chin, and folds of clothes.

Calm Expressions

Peaceful or serious faces

Less exaggerated emotions

Practise tip:

Practice changing eyebrows slightly for different emotions.

Common Beginner Mistakes

Eyes too big (anime style)

Heavy outlines

Stiff poses

Too much shading

Easy Daily Practice Routine (20 Minutes)

5 min – head and face proportions

5 min – eyes and lips

5 min – simple body poses

5 min – hair or clothing flow
