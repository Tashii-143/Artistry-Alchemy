Skills to practise to achieve a Manhwa Artstyle

What is a Manhwa Artstyle?

Manhwa is the Korean comic style. Characters are semi-realistic with pretty faces, smooth lines, and soft coloring. Poses and expressions are natural, and it often looks modern and polished.

Core Skills to Practice (Most Important)

Facial Proportions

Faces are longer than chibi or anime

Eyes are detailed but not huge

Nose and lips are simple but visible

Practise tip:

Draw face guidelines (center line + eye line) before adding details.

Clean Line Art

Thin, neat lines

Very little sketch mess

Practise tip:

Practice slow, confident lines and redraw instead of scribbling.

Stylized Anatomy

Slim bodies

Long legs and arms

Natural poses

Practise tip:

Use stick figures first, then add simple shapes on top.

Expressive Eyes

Eyes show emotion clearly

Often have highlights

Practise tip:

Draw the same face with different eye shapes to show emotions.

Supporting Skills (Learn After Core)

Soft Coloring

Smooth gradients

Natural skin tones

Calm color palettes

Practise tip:

Use one main color and a lighter and darker version.

Simple Shading

Light source is clear

Soft Shadows

Practise tip:

Shade under the chin, hair, and nose only.

Hair Flow

Hair looks soft and flowing

Drawn in sections, not single strands

Practise tip:

Draw hair in big shapes first, then add detail.

Common Beginner Mistakes

Eyes too big (Manga style)

Over-shading

Stiff poses

Too much detail too early

Easy Daily Practice Routine (20 Minutes)

5 min – faces with guidelines

5 min – eyes practice

5 min – simple body poses

5 min – light shading
