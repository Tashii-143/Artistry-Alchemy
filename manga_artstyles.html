Skills to practise to achieve a Manhwa Artstyle

What is a Manga Artstyle?

Manga is the Japanese comic style. It uses clean line art, expressive faces, and simple but powerful drawing techniques. Most manga is black and white and focuses on storytelling and emotions.

Core Skills to Practice (Most Important)

Strong Line Art

Clean, confident lines

Lines can be thick or thin

Practise tip:

Practice drawing long lines without sketching over them.

Facial Expressions

Big emotions

Clear feelings (happy, angry, shocked)

Practise tip:

Draw the same face with different eyebrows and mouths.

Simplified Anatomy

Bodies are stylized

Not super realistic

Practise tip:

Use stick figures first, then add shapes.

Panel Composition (Very Important)

How drawings fit into comic panels

Helps tell a story

Practise tip:

Draw a 3-panel comic with a simple action.

Supporting Skills (After Core)

Screen Tones & Shading

Screen Tones & Shading

Dots and patterns for shadows

Used instead of color

Practise tip:

Shade one side of the face using lines or dots.

Dynamic Poses

Action feels exciting

Characters move a lot

Practise tip:

Practice gesture drawings for 1–2 minutes each.

Hair & Clothing Shapes

Hair is drawn in chunks

Clothes show motion

Practise tip:

Draw hair as big shapes, then add details.

Common Beginner Mistakes

Over-detailing

Making everything realistic

Ignoring panels

Messy line work

Easy Daily Practice Routine (20 Minutes)

5 min – line practice

5 min – faces & expressions

5 min – poses

5 min – simple panels
